Four Purposes of Assessment

 

The ultimate goal of assessing student performance is to improve instruction. The creation of effective assessment tools and the collection of pertinent student data help to produce high-quality instruction. Integrating testing into the instructional process is absolutely essential.
The primary purposes of assessment are for:

·        appraisal,

·        diagnosis,

·        formative evaluation,

·        and summative evaluation.

Appraisal

Assessing students for appraisal  purposes looks at student performance before entry into an instructional sequence. This is sometimes called “Training Needs Assessment”. For example, when you test prospective students for entry into the program, the exam is considered to be an appraisal instrument to decide who is eligible for enrollment. This data may include the student’s present knowledge level, present skill level, prerequisite performance, and capabilities such as math and reading, as well as personality factors. By examining the data, you would be able to identify present student needs and status, and point out where instruction should begin and the direction it should take. These exams could also predict the success of students with a given level of aptitude. By analyzing the entry level of the incoming students, you can tailor your program to meet them where they are and take them to the level you desire for them to be successful apprentices.

Diagnosis

Diagnosis  is similar to appraisal, but it deals with the assessment of the students during the instruction. It is used to determine any difficulties or deficiencies arising during the learning period. Diagnostic materials may be administered to a group or an entire class, but the diagnosis is then used to apply remediation individually in the form of changes to the instruction to meet the needs of students. After the completion of a learning sequence, remediation allows specific teaching to overcome deficiencies identified via diagnosis. Effective teaching requires that you understand the learning process, including the principles of learning, learning styles, and the impact of physical, psychological, and social handicaps on the learning process. You also need to know how to apply diagnostic techniques during training. By using different diagnostic techniques during the learning process, you will be able to make adjustments in your program to correct any problems that may occur.

Formative Evaluation

Formative evaluation  is the third purpose of assessment. This method of educational assessment is conducted on small bodies of content, or on a specific skill for the purpose of determining if the intended objectives have been attained. Decisions about further instruction or revisions to instruction can be based on the results of the formative evaluation that indicate the success or failure of the preceding instruction. The identification of the effectiveness of the instructional process is usually done throughout the training program. The number of questions in the evaluation is determined by the amount or size of the instructional block being measured. Testing for formative evaluation purposes should occur frequently, and may cover material from one lesson, a chapter in a textbook, or material covered over a specified period of time. If the majority of the students do poorly in the evaluation for a specific unit, the instructional sequence or strategy needs to be adjusted. If only a few students do poorly, individualized remediation can address the situation.

Summative Evaluation

Summative evaluation  is defined as a final evaluation of a substantial part of, or all of, the course. It is used to determine if the learner has achieved the ultimate instructional objectives established in advance. These methods report a student’s overall achievement. The data from a summative evaluation can be used to assess the effectiveness of the overall instructional program. Were the objectives obtainable by the students and were the objectives defined clearly enough to serve as measures of achievement? These two questions are usually asked when conducting summative evaluations. Licensing or certification is a type of summative.
